Blogging as Learning

May 1, 2018 by Tom

"I’ve come to realize that what I am best at is not Tai Chi, and it is not chess. What I am best at is the art of learning.”" - Josh Waitzkin

Now that we've taken a look back at the past several months of my journey, it's time to start looking forward. There are several things that I intend to accomplish by keeping up this blog. First and foremost, it will serve as a travelogue through the ups and downs of learning to be a web developer. I'll share my triumphs, my struggles, and all the lessons learned in between. Just to give a quick example, I completed my first weekend assignment from Skill Distillery to build a Cash Register program. It just covered the basics of variables, methods, loops, and if/else logic, but it definitely threw me for a few loops. For the (perhaps morbidly) curious, I'll be including a link to my Github account out on the home page.

This leads to the second big focus of this blog, which is to work alongside my Github portfolio as a way for potential future employers to evaluate my experience and expertise using a variety of different languages, frameworks, and other tools. Although I'm starting from a pretty basic level, it shouldn't be too long before my work starts to get more sophisticated. The mid-term and final projects from SD should be rather advanced pieces of software, and I'm excited about the potential to show off all of our hard work.

Puzzles?

The last big component of this blog might come across as a head scratcher. Did he just say puzzles? Maybe it's the chess nerd in me coming out, but there's something really satisfying about solving a hard puzzle/problem. In chess, that involves finding the best move or series of moves to win material or mate the opponent. In this context, what I want to solve are programming challenges.

Wouldn't you know, there is a gigantic repository of programming problems out on Reddit. The challenges come in three different degrees of difficulty, so I'll be starting with the Easy ones and then moving up if/when I feel ready. One nice thing about these challenges is we'll be able to compare my solution with the top voted solutions from the entire Reddit community to see what I might have missed. I'll try REALLY hard not to cheat and I'll be posting my solution ahead of time and then going back to review the solutions.

Cadence

I'll be updating the blog every Tuesday and Friday, so keep checking back to see the latest content. Each post will be tagged appropriately so that if you're a recruiter, you can get right to the portfolio posts. If you're a solver, you can just check out my puzzle posts. No matter why you're here, I'm glad to have you and I hope you stick around!
